## YOUR TASK — produce the following in order:

### 1. CONCEPT EXTRACTION
Identify 3–5 expandable concepts from the lesson. For each, note:
- The concept name
- Why it deserves a standalone article (not covered deeply in the lesson)
- The cognitive gap it fills (what most people get wrong or misunderstand about it)

### 2. RECOMMENDED FOCUS CONCEPT
Pick the single best concept to expand. Justify your choice based on:
- Topical authority potential (does it anchor a cluster of related searches?)
- Internal linking fit (does it pull readers naturally toward the course?)
- Insight depth (can you say something genuinely new, not just a rewrite of common knowledge?)

### 3. KEYWORD STRATEGY
Propose:
- **Primary keyphrase**: The exact phrase this article should rank for. Choose based on search relevance and natural fit — not predetermined difficulty.
- **Secondary keyphrases** (2–3): Supporting phrases that reinforce topical authority
- **Cannibalization check**: Confirm why this keyphrase does NOT compete with the course page or other likely site pages
- **Search intent label**: Is this informational, investigational, or educational?

### 4. UNIQUE INSIGHT ANGLE
Write 2–3 sentences describing the ONE surprising or counterintuitive insight this article will deliver. This is the "aha moment" that makes the article worth reading, worth citing, and worth ranking. It should expose a common thinking mistake the reader didn't know they were making.

### 5. ARTICLE SKELETON

**Proposed Title** (rankable, clear, not clickbait)
**Meta Description** (under 155 characters, includes primary keyphrase)

**Introduction outline** (3–4 bullet points)
- Hook: the common mistake or misconception
- Why it matters practically
- What the reader will walk away understanding

**Section-by-section outline:**
For each section provide:
- H2 heading
- What this section covers (2–3 sentences)
- The key claim or insight to make here
- Any example, analogy, or case to include
- Internal link opportunity (where relevant — e.g., "link to course lesson on X")

**Conclusion outline**
- What the reader should now believe differently
- CTA: natural transition to the course

### 6. CONTENT DEPTH SIGNALS
List 5–8 specific things this article must include to signal depth to both Google and LLMs:
- A precise definition that corrects a common loose definition
- A real contrast (what this concept IS vs. what people think it is)
- At least one concrete before/after example
- A named framework or mental model (original or attributed)
- Any relevant research, pattern, or observation worth citing
